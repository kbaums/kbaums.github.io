iOS Mobile Applications

AME 494 - Mobile Development

In returning to programming in Swift and Xcode, I wanted to learn more about what makes mobile development different than Mac Development. The most obvious differences were 1) touch screen capabilities and 2) motion sensing technology.


For my final project and honors contract for the class, I developed two gaems. The first was a recreation of my "Subma-ring" game but this time with motion controls and a health bar. The second game uses touch controls to move the earth and save it from being hit by falling asteroids.

Interactive Mac Applications

AME 430 - Mac Development for Media Arts

This was my first introduction to Swift and Xcode. I created interactive media art applications resulting in two projects: a pumpkin face picker and a ring collection game.


To interact with the pumpking face picker, you simply need to drag the mouse across the screen. In "Subma-ring", you control the sub using the WASD keys and try to collect as many rings as you can without floating off of the screen.

Game Development Project

CPI 211 - Game Development II

This project entailed developing a game for the entire semester which consisted of story development, concept art, programming, and 3D modeling. The game is inspired by the ridiculous and hilarious YouTube video "Dog of Wisdom". You play as the small dog in the plane while your friend can play as the Dog of Wisdom in mutliplayer.


The single-player gameplay consists of flying across the sky and shooting down enemies that the Dog of Wisdom sends. At the end of each level, you must answer a riddle in order to continue. The game ends when you complete all of the levels. In multiplayer mode, whoever gains the most points by the end then wins the game.
